Title: A Visual Analytics Platform for Multi-Criteria EV Charging Station Siting and Scenario Exploration

Abstract

Planning urban infrastructure often requires balancing competing objectives such as accessibility, demand, grid capacity, and social equity. Multi-criteria decision analysis is frequently used to support such planning tasks, yet traditional workflows remain largely static and opaque, limiting stakeholders’ ability to explore trade-offs and understand how modelling assumptions influence outcomes.

We present a visual analytics platform that enables interactive exploration of infrastructure planning decisions by integrating multi-criteria suitability analysis with real-time scenario evaluation. The system combines coordinated visual views, including parallel coordinate representations of decision criteria, spatial suitability maps, and diagnostic views that reveal the contribution of individual criteria to ranking outcomes. Users can interactively adjust criterion weights, examine ranking sensitivity, and explore the spatial implications of alternative planning priorities.

Beyond site selection, the platform incorporates a lightweight analytical impact module that estimates potential outcomes of infrastructure deployment, including energy demand, carbon reduction, and socio-demographic coverage. This integration allows planners to evaluate not only where infrastructure should be located but also the potential consequences of proposed interventions.

We demonstrate the approach through a case study of electric vehicle charging infrastructure planning in Greater London using heterogeneous datasets including existing charging locations, census-based socio-economic indicators, accessibility metrics, traffic counts, and electricity network capacity. The results illustrate how interactive visual analytics can support transparent exploration of planning trade-offs and facilitate more informed infrastructure decision making.
